Case Study: Ransomware Attack (WannaCry Incident)

Ransomware is a type of cybercrime where hackers lock your files or your entire system and then ask for money to unlock them. The WannaCry attack in 2017 is an example of this. It spread quickly around the world and affected thousands of computers.

This is what usually happens. The attacker finds a weak spot, like software or tricks someone into clicking on a bad email link or downloading a bad file. When that happens the ransomware installs itself quietly. Then it spreads to computers on the same network. The worst part is that it locks all the files so the user cannot open them. Then a message comes up that says something like, "Pay money in Bitcoin if you want your files back."

The WannaCry attack was very bad. Even hospitals were affected. In the UK many hospitals had to cancel surgeries and appointments because they could not get to data.

The impact of Ransomware Attack can be very serious. People and organizations can lose data, money and trust.. The scary thing is, even after paying money there is no guarantee that the files will be recovered.

This shows how important it is to keep your software updated avoid links and stay alert when you are online because one small mistake can cause big problems, with Ransomware Attack.
